Shipment Value & Freight
Exchange Rate
The BOC uses the BSP (Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas) reference rate at the time of importation. Update this to the current rate.
Transaction Value (PHP) = Invoice Value × Exchange Rate
Use the official BSP rate — not black market or remittance rates.
CIF — Cost + Insurance + Freight
The Philippines uses the CIF valuation method as the basis for computing customs duties. This means:
CIF Value = Transaction Value (PHP) + Freight + Insurance
Customs Duty is then computed based on this CIF Value, not just the invoice price. This is mandated under the CMTA (Customs Modernization and Tariff Act, RA 10863).
Tariff & Tax Rates
Value Added Tax (VAT)
All imported goods are subject to 12% VAT under the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC), as amended by the TRAIN Law.
VAT Base = CIF + Customs Duty + Excise Tax + Other Charges
VAT = VAT Base × 12%
The VAT is computed on top of the dutiable value plus all applicable taxes — this is called the landed cost.
Additional Fees
Port Charges
Arrastre — fee for handling goods within the port/pier area. Charged per metric ton or per unit.
Wharfage — fee for use of wharf/pier facilities. Also computed per metric ton.
These are collected by the PPA (Philippine Ports Authority) or private port operators. They are included in the VAT base computation.
Typical Arrastre: PHP 500–2,000/CBM
Typical Wharfage: PHP 50–300/MT
